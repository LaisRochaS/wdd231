Winter Hiking Safety

Staying safe is the most important part of your winter hiking adventure. Here are the key tips to help you prepare and stay protected in snowy conditions.

Check Weather Conditions

Always check local weather forecasts before heading out. Sudden storms, extreme cold, or heavy snowfall can make trails dangerous or inaccessible.

Dress in Layers

Wear moisture-wicking base layers, insulating mid-layers, and waterproof outerwear. Avoid cotton and always bring a hat, gloves, and extra socks.

Carry the Essentials

Bring a map, compass, flashlight/headlamp, extra food, water, first aid kit, emergency blanket, and a whistle. Don't rely only on your phone.

Tell Someone Your Plan

Let someone know where you're going and when you expect to return. Cell service is often unreliable in mountainous or forested areas.

Watch for Hypothermia & Frostbite

Know the signs of hypothermia (shivering, confusion, slurred speech) and frostbite (numbness, pale or hard skin). Take breaks and stay dry.
